# Sutra 4 — Subtle Sound Phonics: Nāda, Bindu, and Esoteric Centers

## 1. Sanskrit

Devanāgarī:
नादबिन्दु-मयं वापि किं चन्द्रार्ध-निरोधिकाः ।
चक्रारूढमनच्कं वा किं वा शक्ति-स्वरूपकम् ॥ ४ ॥

IAST:
nādabindu-mayaṃ vāpi kiṃ candrārdha-nirodhikāḥ |
cakrārūḍhamanackaṃ vā kiṃ vā śakti-svarūpakam || 4 ||

## 3. Open translation

Or does Reality consist of the resonance of sound (Nāda) and the concentrated point (Bindu)? Or is it the crescent moon (Candrārdha) and the impeder (Nirodhikā)? Or is it the vowelless sound placed within the energy centers (Cakra)? Or is it the very essence of Shakti?

## 4. Literal reading

The Goddess asks if Reality consists of the sound vibration (*nāda*) and the point of focus (*bindu*), or the crescent moon shape (*candrārdha*) and the psychic blocks/obstructions (*nirodhikā*), or if it is the sound without vowels (*anacka*) placed on the energy centers (*cakra*), or if it is the true form of Energy/Shakti itself.

## 5. Philosophical meaning

This verse references the subtle yogic stages of **Uccāra** (the upward movement of breath and sound along the central channel):
- **Nāda & Bindu**: *Nāda* is the cosmic sound resonance; *Bindu* is the condensed, singular point of consciousness/light.
- **Candrārdha & Nirodhikā**: As sound ascends through the central channel towards the crown, it undergoes stages of refinement. *Candrārdha* (crescent moon) is a state of extremely subtle vibration. *Nirodhikā* (the impeder) is a threshold where the sound becomes so dense and subtle that it seems to block further ascent, requiring total surrender to pass.
- **Cakrārūḍham Anackam**: *Anacka* refers to a consonant sound without any vowel (a raw, silent phoneme or seed sound) sounded as it is mounted or focused upon the *cakras* (wheels of the energy body).
- **Śakti-svarūpakam**: She asks if the ultimate reality is simply Shakti (Energy) itself in its pure form.

## 6. Practice instruction

1. Sit comfortably. Close your eyes and ears with your fingers (Shanmukhi Mudra).
2. Listen to the internal sounds inside your head (the *Anāhata Nāda*). It might sound like a high-pitched hum, a bell, or rushing water.
3. Gently focus on the point where the sound appears to emerge from (the *Bindu*).
4. Do not try to analyze or change the sound. Merely watch the shift in your state as the mind becomes absorbed in the sound.
5. Ask yourself: "Am I the sound, or am I the one who is aware of the sound?"

## 8. Key concepts

- **nāda**: Inner mystical sound vibration.
- **bindu**: Condensed drop or seed of consciousness.
- **candrārdha**: The crescent-moon stage of sound ascent.
- **nirodhikā**: The energetic threshold near the third eye/crown.
- **anacka**: Vowelless, unmanifest sound.

## 9. Cross-references

- **Shiva Sutras 2.3**: *vidyāśarīrasattā mantrarahasyam* (The secret of mantra is the manifestation of the body of knowledge).
- **Spanda Kārikā 2.3**: Explaining the role of the energetic centers in binding or releasing consciousness.

## 10. Scholarly notes

- Jaideva Singh notes that these sound stages (*nāda*, *bindu*, *candrārdha*, *nirodhikā*, etc.) represent the standard scale of sound dissolution in Trika yoga, heading up to the crown (*dvādaśānta*) [singh1979vijnanabhairava].
- Swami Lakshmanjoo describes *anacka* as the sounding of a consonant like 'K' or 'T' without any vowel support, which forces the breath to stop and the mind to enter the central channel (*suṣumṇā*) [lakshmanjoo2007vijnana].
- Christopher Wallis notes that *nirodhikā* acts as a gatekeeper that forces the yogin to transition from effort-based yoga (*āṇavopāya*) to grace-based surrender (*śāktopāya*) [wallis2018vbt].
- Osho observes that experiences of inner sound (Nāda) and condensed light-points (Bindu) are genuine milestones of subtle energy activation in deep meditation. However, they are still phenomenological events within consciousness. The absolute is not the sound itself, but the witnessing presence that is aware of these phenomena [osho1998bookofsecrets].

## 11. Practice cautions

When practicing listening to internal sounds, do not strain or create pressure in the ears. If you feel dizzy, stop the practice and rest.
