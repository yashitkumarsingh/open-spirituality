# Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra (VBT)

A scholarly, visual, practice-aware, and citation-first study of the Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra.

This module provides Sanskrit texts, transliterations, word-by-word grammatical breakdowns, original open translations, philosophical commentaries, visual maps, and contemplative practices for each verse of the Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra.

## Context & Overview

The *Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra* (c. 850 CE) is a foundational text of the nondual Trika system of Kashmir Śaivism. Structured as a dialogue between the Goddess Bhairavī (Śakti) and the God Bhairava (Śiva), it opens with the Goddess expressing doubts about the ultimate nature of Reality (verses 1–22). 

Bhairava responds that ultimate reality is not found in external rituals, forms, or conceptual divisions, but is the formless, all-pervading consciousness (verses 23-24). He then reveals **112 contemplative methods** or meditation gateways (*dhāraṇās*) to directly recognize this reality (verses 24–163).
