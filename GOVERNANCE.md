# Project Governance

The **Open Spirituality** project is a decentralized, consensus-driven community dedicated to creating high-quality, open-source spiritual knowledge systems. 

## Governance Principles

1. **Decentralized Stewardship**: No single individual or lineage "owns" the repository. Decisions are made collaboratively by the community of contributors.
2. **Scholarly Consensus**: Disagreements regarding translation, word breakdowns, and philosophical interpretation are resolved through citation-first arguments. When multiple traditional views exist, our policy is to document all prominent views rather than select one as "correct."
3. **Lineage Respect & Pluralism**: We treat traditional lineages and schools with respect. Traditional views are presented clearly alongside modern comparative readings.
4. **Peer Review**: Every modification to the base text, grammar tables, translations, or practices must go through a formal GitHub Pull Request review.

## Role Descriptions

### Contributors
Anyone who submits a pull request, opens an issue, reviews code/content, or participates in discussions is a contributor.

### Maintainers
Maintainers are trusted contributors who have demonstrated scholarly and technical competence, lineage sensitivity, and commitment to the project. They have write access to the repository and are responsible for merging pull requests and moderating discussions.
- Maintainers must ensure all merged content complies with the **no copyright pollution** rule.
- Maintainers are expected to act in accordance with the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Decision Making Process

For minor edits (typos, grammar corrections, schema fixes), any maintainer can merge after review.

For major content additions (new translations, philosophical explanations, visual maps):
- The PR must be open for review for at least **5 days** to allow Sanskritists and practitioners to comment.
- At least **two** other contributors or maintainers must sign off on the PR.
- Disagreements must be settled through citations of canonical materials (using references cited in the text-specific bibliography databases).
