# Sanskrit Source & Translation Policy — Ashtavakra Gita

To maintain the high academic and ethical standards of **Open Spirituality**, this module operates under a strict clean-room translation policy.

## 1. Sourcing Base Sanskrit Text

- **Primary Source**: The Sanskrit base text for this module is sourced from the **1940 edition of the Ashtavakra Samhita**, edited and translated by **Swami Nityaswarupananda** and published by **Advaita Ashrama (Mayavati)**. 
- **Public Domain Status**: Because this edition was published in 1940, the Sanskrit base text and standard English definitions are in the public domain globally.
- **Verification**: All Sanskrit verses are cross-verified against standard digitizations (such as the Sanskrit Documents library and Gita Press editions) to correct minor typographical or print errors.

## 2. Intellectual Property (IP) Boundaries

- **No Copyright Violations**: We do **NOT** copy or reproduce copyrighted modern translations or commentaries of the Ashtavakra Gita (e.g., Swami Chinmayananda, Thomas Byrom, Bart Marshall, Swami Tejomayananda, etc.).
- **Reference-Only Policy**: These modern translations are studied strictly as comparison resources to resolve linguistic discrepancies. They are cited in the scholarly notes using their BibTeX keys from `bibliography.bib` and are never copied.
- **Original Content**: All "Open Translations," word-by-word grammatical tables, and philosophical commentaries generated in this module are original works created specifically for this repository.

## 3. Orthography & Transliteration Standards

- **Devanagari**: Written using standard Devanagari Unicode.
- **IAST Transliteration**: Standard International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration (IAST) is used, with strict adherence to diacritic marks (e.g., *ā*, *ī*, *ū*, *ṛ*, *ś*, *ṣ*, *ṭ*, *ḍ*, *ṇ*, *ṃ*, *ḥ*).
- **Sandhi Splitting**: In the continuous Sanskrit files, the original combined sandhi is preserved. In the word-by-word grammar tables, sandhis are cleanly split into individual grammatical units to assist student comprehension.
